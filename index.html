AI in perspective

The bigger picture: One person's AI use is tiny next to driving, flying, or eating. But the totals add up at global scale — the world's data centres used roughly 415 TWh of electricity in 2024 (about 1.5% of global supply), and the IEA projects that could roughly double by 2030. The individual choices that move the needle most are still how you travel, heat your home, and eat.
Estimates are illustrative and use published average emission factors; your real footprint depends on local grid mix, occupancy, and behaviour. Weights shown in US short tons / pounds, water in gallons.
